Congratulations on your expected new arrival! Thank you for stopping by. If you are an expectant parent, you know you’re in the right place if…

  • You view the task ahead of you as both exciting and terrifying at the same time
  • You want more out of your pregnancy and childbirth experience than the advice and anecdotes that you’re suddenly being bombarded with from every direction
  • You see pregnancy, childbirth and parenthood as an opportunity for self-growth and discovery with the potential to shape you as both a person and as a parent

Helping you navigate these waters from bump to new baby is where I come in. Whether you’re a soon-to-be-mom (or you love a soon-to-be-mom), my purpose is to wholeheartedly support you throughout your birth experience so that you can feel nurtured, confident and empowered to embrace this new role as a parent and your baby with open arms.

Prenatal Care

  • Complimentary meet and greet to see if we are a good fit.
  • 2 prenatal visits to discuss questions and/or concerns you may have as well as your birth wishes and vision.
  • Unlimited support via text, phone or email throughout pregnancy

Labor and Delivery

  • 24/7 on-call availability in the weeks surrounding your due date
  • Continuous labor support tailored to your particular needs and desires
  • Up to two hours support after birth to provide breastfeeding support and encourage bonding.

Postpartum Follow-Up

Finally, I offer one postpartum visit in your first weeks home with baby to check-in on how everyone is settling in as well as mom and baby’s well-being and to assist you in processing your birth experience.

What Is a Doula?

A birth doula is a person who assists women and their partners in preparing for childbirth by providing emotional support, information as well as physical comfort to a woman before, during and just after childbirth. The Greek word Doula means woman caregiver or more literally, woman servant and is used today to describe a trained labor companion.

Why Have a Doula?

Throughout history in cultures around the globe, women have given birth accompanied by other knowledgeable, experienced women whose strength, comfort and nurturing have not only helped bring forth new generations of infants but also confident, capable mothers. The positive outcomes of using a birth doula are evidence based and highly supported by research. With continuous support from a Doula, women experienced

  • 31% decrease in the use of Pitocin
  • 28% decrease in the risk of Cesarean
  • 12% increase in the likelihood of a spontaneous vaginal birth
  • 9% decrease in the use of any medications for pain relief
  • 14% decrease in the risk of newborns being admitted to a special care nursery
  • 34% decrease in the risk of being dissatisfied with the birth experience

About Me

JenniferGray

After being captivated by my own pregnancy, childbirth and subsequent transition into motherhood, I felt deeply drawn to assure other birthing mothers unconditional support throughout their own childbirth experiences. In my journey toward becoming a Doula I have come to believe that a woman who has been well cared for during her pregnancy, labor and childbirth (whose desires have been listened to and whose physical sensations and emotions affirmed) is in turn, better prepared to care for and nurture her child(ren).

A Midwestern girl all my life, I graduated with my Master’s in Social Work and Mental Health from the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign. After the birth of my son in 2016, a new passion for birth and supporting birthing women convinced me to direct the knowledge gained from my previous educational pursuits into what I hope will be a long career of providing education and reliable support to families in perhaps the greatest transition of their lives…that of welcoming new life!
